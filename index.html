01.

About Me

Born and raised in Montreal, Rubin Gruber is a serial entrepreneur with over 40 years of experience pioneering technologies that transformed global telecommunications. His career began at BBN Communications, where he drove next-generation packet-switching technology for mission-critical applications.

As co-founder of Sonus Networks (1997), Rubin helped revolutionize voice-over-IP — enabling telephone companies to route voice calls over data networks at a fraction of the cost of traditional circuit switching. He later founded Verivue, Inc. (2006), a content delivery network company that was acquired by Akamai Technologies in 2012.

Today, Rubin serves as Chairman of Metrika, Inc., leading blockchain monitoring and analytics that give enterprises end-to-end visibility into decentralized networks. He is also a dedicated mentor and philanthropist, supporting the next generation of innovators through scholarships and academic programs at McGill University and Florida Atlantic University.

Professional Experience

2019 – PRESENT

Chairman — Metrika, Inc.

Leads a blockchain monitoring and analytics platform providing enterprises with end-to-end visibility into decentralized networks, enabling informed decision-making across the evolving digital asset landscape.

2006 – 2012

Founder — Verivue, Inc.

Founded a cloud-edge infrastructure company delivering on-demand television, movies, and content distribution at scale. Verivue was acquired by Akamai Technologies (NASDAQ: AKAM) in 2012, validating its position as a leading CDN innovator.

1997 – 2004

Co-Founder & Chairman — Sonus Networks (NASDAQ: SONS)

Co-founded one of the pioneering voice-over-IP companies, building packet switches that allowed telephone carriers to replace expensive circuit-switching infrastructure. Sonus became a Nasdaq-listed company and a global leader in IP telecommunications.

1970s – 1979

Founder — Cambridge Telecommunications (CTX)

Founded CTX, the first company to use semiconductors to connect people to networks — a breakthrough that predated and anticipated the digital networking revolution. CTX was acquired by GTE in 1979, cementing its place as a foundational chapter in the history of modern telecommunications.
